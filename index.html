Coaching Philosophy

How I coach

I coach close to the work.

01 Coach the pattern, not just the person +
I look at the calls, emails, escalations, patterns, and pressure points. Then I ask: is this a people issue, a training issue, a process issue, a capacity issue, or a leadership issue?

If one person is struggling, we coach the person. If five people are struggling with the same thing, we fix the system.
Customer Experience

How I think about customer experience

Speed matters, but speed without care creates repeat work.

01 Customers should leave feeling safe, guided, and confident +
A good customer journey should feel clear. The customer should know what is happening, what happens next, who owns it, and when they will hear back.

The goal is not just for the customer to leave satisfied. The goal is for them to leave feeling safe, guided, and confident that the right people have them.

Operational Awareness

What I pay attention to

In high-volume service environments, I watch the small things because they usually become the big things.

Are customers calling back because nobody followed up properly?
Are agents guessing because the process is unclear?
Are Team Leads coaching, or just putting out fires?
Are the same issues showing up again and again?

That is where better service starts.

Situational Thinking

How I think in practice

When pressure hits, this is how I diagnose and respond.

01 When volume is rising +
I look at call queues, email volume, ticket trends, response times, staffing coverage, and repeat contacts. High volume does not always mean "work harder." Sometimes it means the workflow, routing, ownership, or coverage model needs to be fixed.
02 When performance drops +
I look for patterns before blame. Is it coaching? Training? Tools? Motivation? Capacity? Unclear expectations? The answer matters because the wrong diagnosis creates the wrong solution.
03 When customers are frustrated +
I focus on ownership, next steps, tone, and follow-through. People calm down faster when they feel someone capable has taken the wheel.
04 When Team Leads need support +
I help leaders move from reacting to leading. That means clearer expectations, better coaching habits, stronger documentation, and more confidence in difficult conversations.
05 When capacity needs planning +
I look at volume trends, forecasts, coverage gaps, team availability, and hiring needs early. The goal is to stay ahead of demand before the team is overwhelmed and the customer experience starts slipping.
06 When Sales Operations needs alignment +
I connect strategy to daily execution. A strong plan only works when the team understands the goal, the workflow supports it, and the metrics show whether it is actually being met.
